Summer Research Project (2021)

Supervisor: Paul McMillan

In Gaia Early Data Release 3, a clear break in the angular momentum-vertical velocity space can be seen (Fig. 15 in Antoja et al. 2020), a break that is suspected to have a dynamical origin. I explore this possible origin by doing N-body simulations (with more than 3 million particles) of how a Milky Way-like galaxy evolves after a Sagittarius dwarf impact, using the RAMSES code. The project was done in collaboration with Joss Bland-Hawthorn and Thor Tepper-García. I use a similar numerical set-up for the simulations, and the same code to generate initial conditions for a Milky Way-like galaxy as they use in Bland-Hawthorn & Tepper-García (2021).


Bachelor Project (2020)

Title: Capture of interstellar objects in the Solar system
Supervisor: Daohai Li
Publication: LUP Student Papers

Through N-body simulations using the MERCURY code (Chambers, 1999), I determine the capture rate of interstellar objects in the Solar system and orbital features of captured objects. I also look into how interstellar objects are captured and the contribution each planet in the Solar system has on the overall capture rate.
